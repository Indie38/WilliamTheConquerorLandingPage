Welcome to RMTi

Rhythmic Movement Training International is a collation of exercises that improve symmetry in the brain. Movements are designed to stimulate neural pathways, improving concentration, focus and organisation. It has also proven to be effective in enhancing emotional self-regulation, including reduction in anxiety as well as reducing dificulty in auditory and visual processing. RMTi is particularly successful in aiding development of children, strengthening balance and resilience.

History of RMTi

RMTi is based on the practices of the Swedish photographer Kerstin Linde and her observations of infant's development.

Information About RMTi


Who am I?

My name’s Claire. I’m a mother and became desperate for help for one of my two children. He struggled with concentration, coordination, organisation, and couldn’t sit still in school. I was aware that this could be detrimental to both his education and development into an adolescent. Determined to do whatever I could to help my son, I searched for a solution. That’s when I found RMTi. After just two months, there were significant improvements in his behaviour, being much calmer and able to maintain concentration in school. I’ve never looked back. Fast forward ten years and I am a qualified RMTi Practitioner myself. I love helping children to improve their physical and mental wellbeing. I also use RMTi’s calming strategies in my “main” job as a teacher. I continue to see the benefits of RMTi among my family and friends, finding it of significant importance during the covid pandemic.


More Benefits

As well as improving general well being and organisation, RMTi has also been shown to help with conditions such as: autism, ADHD and emotional imbalances without the need for additional medication. It’s a natural approach to strengthen children and adult's development.


How does RMTi work?

Rhythmic Movement Training International (RMTi) is a neuro-development therapy. It improves synchronisation between the brain and body to create new, efficient neural pathways, strengthening myelination of neurons. It is a gentle, movement-based program bespoke to each client. The movements stimulate the brain, prompting learning of new reflex patterns and ultimately forms a calmer, more regulated way of being, helping patients feel better equipped to face the world.
